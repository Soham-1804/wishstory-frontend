five years
01
year one · 2020
🌱

The arguments were loud. The making-up was louder. I learned you need exactly fifteen minutes of silence before you can receive an apology. You learned I am completely unreachable before 9am and to stop trying.

"We were terrible at this. We got better. We're still getting better."

02
year two · 2021
🌊

Goa. The food poisoning. The monsoon nobody warned us about. Dancing on the terrace at 1am in the rain because we'd already paid for the room and might as well. Nowhere to be. I didn't want to be anywhere else.

"You still haven't admitted the restaurant was your idea."

03
year three · 2022
🕯️

The hard one. The year I understood what choosing someone actually costs. You stayed when leaving would have been so much easier. I have thought about that almost every day since. I don't take it for granted.

"I almost didn't. You know that. Thank you for knowing that and staying anyway."

04
year four · 2023
🌸

We found our rhythm. Sunday mornings. A playlist we both kept updating but neither of us admitted to. The way you look at me across a crowded room like I'm the only fixed point. I was always looking back.

"I added 'Tum Ho' to the playlist six months ago. You haven't mentioned it."

05
year five · today
✨

Half a decade of you. And still — when you call, I pick up faster than for anyone else. When you walk in, something in me settles. I love you more precisely now. More knowingly. More completely than I knew was possible five years ago in that café when you were fifteen minutes late and entirely unapologetic.

"Happy anniversary, Meera. I'd be twenty-two minutes early all over again."

the letter
"
Meera —

Five years ago I had no idea I was about to meet someone who would quietly, entirely, change what I thought my life was supposed to look like.

You didn't sweep me off my feet. You did something better — you gave me somewhere to land. You made the ordinary feel like something. The Tuesday evenings. The half-read books on the table."The specific way you say my name when you're about to tell me something that matters." I've noticed all of it. Every single thing.

I have written this letter in my head a hundred times. On trains. In the shower. In at least four meetings I should have been paying attention to. None of those versions were good enough. This one probably isn't either. But it's real, and everything I feel for you is real, and some things are worth saying badly.

Thank you for staying in year three. Thank you for the playlist. Thank you for being fifteen minutes late that January and for that being the first thing I ever knew about you.

Thank you for the version of myself I get to be when I'm with you. I didn't know that version existed before you.

all of it, all of me —
Karthik 💌
